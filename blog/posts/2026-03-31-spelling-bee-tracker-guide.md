# Spelling Bee Tracker: Never Miss Another Word on Your Way to Queen Bee

If you're chasing Genius or Queen Bee on the NYT Spelling Bee, you know the pain. You've found 30 words, you're stuck, and the hint grid is taunting you with numbers you can't crack. You know there's a 5-letter word starting with "P" that you're missing, but you just can't see it.

That's why I built the [Spelling Bee Tracker](https://tylervibes.com/spelling-bee-guide/).

## What Is It?

The Spelling Bee Tracker is a progress-tracking tool that works alongside the NYT Spelling Bee hint grid. You paste in the hint grid data, log the words you've found, and the tracker shows you exactly what's left — broken down by starting letter and word length.

## How It Works

### Step 1: Paste the Hint Grid

The NYT Spelling Bee publishes a hint grid every day (available on various Spelling Bee hint sites). It's a table that shows how many words exist for each starting letter and word length. Copy that grid and paste it into the tracker.

### Step 2: Log Your Found Words

As you find words in the puzzle, type them into the tracker. It validates them against the hint grid and updates your progress in real time.

### Step 3: See What's Missing

This is where it gets good. The tracker shows:

- **The full grid** with found words crossed off and remaining counts highlighted
- **Two-letter combos** — how many words start with each two-letter pair (e.g., "PR: 3 remaining")
- **Your total progress** toward Genius and Queen Bee

The two-letter combo list is the killer feature. The NYT gives you this data in their hints, and seeing "BA: 2, BE: 1, BI: 0, BO: 3" instantly focuses your search. You stop guessing randomly and start targeting specific letter combinations.

## Why It's Better Than a Notepad

Sure, you could track this on paper. But:

- **The grid updates automatically.** Find a word, type it in, and the counts adjust instantly.
- **You can see patterns.** When you notice that all your remaining words are 7+ letters starting with "S", you know exactly where to focus.
- **No mental math.** The tracker does the subtraction so you don't have to count what's left in each row.
- **Remove mistakes.** Accidentally log a word that doesn't count? Just remove it with one click.

## Tips for Hitting Queen Bee

After using this tool for months, here's what I've found works:

- **Work through the two-letter list systematically.** Don't jump around — pick a combo and exhaust it.
- **Think about word endings.** "-TION", "-MENT", "-NESS", "-LING" — these patterns unlock words you'd never think of by starting letter alone.
- **Don't forget compound-ish words.** The Spelling Bee accepts some surprisingly long words that are just two common words stuck together.
- **Come back later.** Seriously. Take a break, come back in an hour, and you'll immediately see words you were blind to before.
